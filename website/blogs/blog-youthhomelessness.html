What is Youth Homelessness?

Youth homelessness happens when young people, usually between the ages of 12 and 24, don’t have a safe place to live. This could mean sleeping on the streets, in shelters, or even couch surfing at a friend’s place because they have nowhere else to go.

Imagine trying to go to school, work a job, or plan your future without knowing where you'll sleep tonight. That’s the reality for many young people.

Why Does It Happen?

There isn’t just one reason. Here are a few common ones:

  • Family problems: Some young people leave home because of abuse, neglect, or big fights at home.
  • Poverty: When families can’t afford housing, young people might end up on their own.
  • LGBTQ+ rejection: Some young people are kicked out because of who they are or who they love.
  • Aging out of foster care: When teens in foster care turn 18, they often don’t have anywhere to go.

What Happens to Homeless Youth?

Without a stable home, life gets really hard. Homeless youth are more likely to:

  • Miss school or drop out
  • Struggle with mental health
  • Face violence and abuse on the streets
  • Get sick more often

But here’s the thing: they’re just like anyone else. They have dreams, talents, and hopes for the future. They just need a little help.

How Can We Help?

You don’t have to be rich or famous to make a difference. Here’s what you can do:

  • Learn more: The more we know, the more we can help.
  • Speak up: Talk about it with your friends and family. Awareness matters.
  • Support local shelters: Donate clothes, food, or your time if you can.
  • Be kind: A smile or a friendly word can mean a lot to someone going through a tough time.

Everyone Deserves a Safe Place

Nobody should have to face the world alone, especially not young people. If we work together, we can build a world where every young person has a safe place to sleep, grow, and dream.
