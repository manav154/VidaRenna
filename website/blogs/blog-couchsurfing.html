What is Couch Surfing?

Couch surfing is when someone sleeps at different people’s homes (on couches, floors, or spare beds) because they don’t have a home of their own. It’s often thought of as “not that bad” because they’re not on the streets. But here’s the truth:

Couch surfing is still homelessness.

Young people who couch surf often move from place to place, staying with friends, relatives, or even strangers. They might not know where they’ll sleep next week—or even tomorrow.

Why Do Young People Couch Surf?

There are lots of reasons why a young person might end up couch surfing:

  • They had to leave home because it wasn’t safe.
  • Their family can’t afford rent or lost their housing.
  • They aged out of foster care and don’t have support.
  • They came out as LGBTQ+ and were rejected by their family.

Many don’t want to be “a burden,” so they move around quietly, trying not to be noticed. That’s why couch surfing youth are sometimes called the hidden homeless.

Why It’s a Problem

Couch surfing might seem better than sleeping outside but it’s still really tough. It can mean:

  • No privacy or personal space
  • Missing school or work because of moving around
  • Feeling unsafe or uncomfortable in someone else’s home
  • Struggling with stress, anxiety, or depression

It’s hard to focus on your future when you don’t even know where you’ll sleep.

What Can We Do?

Here are a few ways we can help young people who are couch surfing:

  • Don’t judge: If someone opens up to you, listen and support them.
  • Support local shelters and services: They provide safe places and resources.
  • Raise awareness: Many people don’t realize couch surfing is a form of homelessness.
  • Be kind and inclusive: Sometimes just knowing someone cares can make a big difference.

Everyone Deserves Stability

Couch surfing might sound temporary, but for many young people, it’s a long and uncertain journey. No one should have to live without stability, safety, or support.

Let’s look out for each other and make sure no one is left without support, especially those simply trying to find a safe place to sleep.
