When Family Isn’t Safe

Sadly, not every home is filled with love. Some young people grow up in homes with:

  • Constant yelling, fighting, or violence
  • Parents who struggle with addiction or mental health issues
  • Neglect, where basic needs like food, clothes, or safety are ignored
  • Emotional, physical, or sexual abuse
  • Rejection for being LGBTQ+, different, or just for standing up for themselves

When things get this bad, home isn’t a safe place anymore. In fact, it becomes dangerous.

Why Do They Leave?

Some young people run away because they feel scared, unwanted, or completely alone. Others get kicked out by their families. Either way, they often leave with nothing, meaning no money, no plan, and no one to turn to.

It’s not about being “bad” or “acting out.” It’s about surviving.

Life After Leaving Home

Without support, these young people may end up:

  • Sleeping in shelters, on the streets, or couch surfing
  • Struggling with school or work
  • Facing hunger, violence, or health problems
  • Feeling isolated, anxious, or depressed

They didn’t choose this life, rather they were forced into it by circumstances no one should have to go through.

What Can We Do?

Here’s how we can help:

  • Believe and listen: If someone shares their story, believe them. It takes courage to speak up.
  • Support organizations: Shelters, hotlines, and youth programs save lives.
  • Raise awareness: Many people don’t know this is happening right in their communities.
  • Create safe spaces: Whether you’re a friend, teacher, neighbor, or mentor, showing kindness and offering help matters more than you know.

Every Young Person Deserves Safety

No young person should have to leave home to feel safe. Every young person deserves care, support, and a future without fear. Let’s speak up, support them, and work toward a world where every youth has a safe place to call home.
